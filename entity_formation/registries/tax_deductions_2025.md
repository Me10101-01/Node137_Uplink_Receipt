## Tax Optimization Strategies

### 1. Section 179 Expensing
- Immediately expense qualifying equipment up to $1,160,000 (2025)
- Deduct full cost in year of purchase vs. depreciation
- Applies to computers, servers, networking, software

### 2. Home Office Deduction
- Exclusive and regular business use required
- Calculate: (Office sq ft / Home sq ft) × Total home expenses
- Simplified option: $5/sq ft up to 300 sq ft ($1,500 max)

### 3. Retirement Contributions
- Solo 401(k): Up to $69,000 (2025) for self-employed
- SEP IRA: Up to 25% of compensation
- SIMPLE IRA: $16,000 + $3,500 catch-up (if 50+)

### 4. Health Insurance
- Self-employed health insurance deduction
- HSA contributions: $4,300 (individual) or $8,550 (family) (2025)

### 5. S-Corporation Election (SSSF LLC)
- Potential self-employment tax savings
- Reasonable salary requirement
- Payroll compliance costs
- Consult CPA for cost/benefit analysis

## Important Reminders

### Record Retention
- **Tax Returns:** Permanent
- **Supporting Documents:** 7 years (IRS audit period)
- **Asset Records:** Until disposed + 7 years
- **Payroll Records:** 4 years (FLSA requirement)

### Audit Risk Factors
- Home office deduction (common trigger)
- Large equipment expenses
- High business expense ratio
- Crypto transactions (increasing scrutiny)
- Multi-entity structure (ensure proper documentation)

### Best Practices
- Never commingle personal and business expenses
- Maintain separate bank accounts for each entity
- Document business purpose for all expenses
- Keep digital and physical copies of receipts
- Use business credit cards for all business expenses
