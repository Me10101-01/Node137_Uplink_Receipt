## Tax Classification Options

### Default Classification (Disregarded Entity)
Single-member LLCs are automatically classified as "disregarded entities" for tax purposes, meaning income/expenses flow through to owner's personal tax return (Schedule C).

### S-Corporation Election
File Form 2553 to elect S-corporation tax treatment:
- **Advantages:** Potential self-employment tax savings
- **Disadvantages:** Payroll requirements, additional compliance
- **Deadline:** Within 75 days of formation or by March 15 of tax year

### C-Corporation Election
File Form 8832 to elect C-corporation tax treatment:
- **Advantages:** Separate tax entity, potential tax deferral
- **Disadvantages:** Double taxation on distributions
- **Use Case:** Generally not recommended for small software companies

**Recommendation:** Consult with CPA to determine optimal tax structure based on projected revenue and business goals.

## Important Reminders

### Security
- **Never share EIN publicly** - treat like a SSN
- Store in secure credential vault (1Password: SSSF LLC vault)
- Limit access to authorized personnel only
- Use only on official government and banking forms

### Verification
- IRS does not call or email to request EIN information
- Beware of EIN-related scams
- Verify all communications directly with IRS

### Updates
- Notify IRS of any changes to business structure
- Update responsible party if ownership changes
- File final return when business dissolves
